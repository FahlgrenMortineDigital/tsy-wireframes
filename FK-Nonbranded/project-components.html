Used on: All content pages FDS: Content Block / Text

Safe Practices for Parenteral Nutrition

Parenteral nutrition is classified as a high-alert medication due to its complex formulation, narrow therapeutic index, and potential for serious adverse events when administered incorrectly. Ensuring patient safety requires adherence to standardized protocols covering order verification, compounding, labeling, administration, and ongoing monitoring throughout the course of PN therapy.
